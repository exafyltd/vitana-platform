## What it is

The Vitana Index is your single wellness number. It's a 0-999 score built from five canonical pillars — Nutrition, Hydration, Exercise, Sleep, Mental — multiplied by a balance factor that rewards consistency across all five. Each pillar caps at 200, the unweighted total caps at 1000, and the balance factor (between 0.7 and 1.0) is what turns a lopsided 950 into a real 760 and a balanced 700 into a genuine 700.

The Index updates every time you log something — a glass of water, a walk, a journal entry, a sleep session — or when a connected tracker syncs. It is the headline number you see in the Home header, on the Health overview, and in the morning brief.

## Why it matters

The Index is how Vitana knows whether anything it suggests is actually working. If you log water for a week and the score doesn't move, the suggestion was wrong. If you take a walk and Hydration jumps four points, you have evidence. The number isn't a grade — it's a feedback loop that tells you which interventions land for *your* body.

It also lets Vitana trade priorities honestly. The Autopilot ranks tomorrow's actions by which one is likely to move the Index the most given your current weakest pillar and your active Life Compass goal. No score = no ranking = recommendations that drift.

## How to use it

1. Look at the score in the Home header at least once a day — it's the temperature gauge.
2. When something feels off, open Vitana Index Detail. The five pillar bars show you which one is dragging.
3. Read the **balance factor** — if it's below 0.85, you have a strong pillar masking a weak one. The fastest score gain is to fix the weak pillar, not push the strong one harder.
4. The **tier ladder** at the bottom (Starting / Early / Building / Steady / Strong / Thriving) is the long-game frame. Tier-up is the right milestone to celebrate; the daily score is just the temperature.
5. To make the score move, log via the Diary or connect a tracker. See concept 0.2 (the Five Pillars) for what each pillar measures.
