## What it is

Did You Know is the 30-day guided tour Vitana runs the first month you use the system. Every day a small card appears on Home with one new feature, one new concept, or one new way to use Vitana — Index-centric, ranked by what would help you most given your survey baseline. ORB also offers each tour item as a voice opener; if you answer "yes" you get a one-minute spoken explanation. The tour is silent if you ignore it (no notification spam) and ends naturally after the 30-day window or when you've covered the high-value items.

## Why it matters

Vitana has dozens of features — far more than anyone discovers by clicking around. Without a guided tour, you'd reach for the same three screens for a year. Did You Know is calibrated to your *usage*, not the calendar — if you skip days, the tour pauses, so the 30 cards stretch over real time spent in the app. The ordering is also adaptive: cards that point at your weakest pillar surface first; cards about features you've already used surface later or get skipped.

## Where to find it

- **Home (`/home`)** — the Did You Know card sits in the morning brief area
- **ORB voice** — when ORB opens a session, you may hear "Did you know you can dictate your diary?" as a soft offer
- A toggle in **Settings → Preferences** lets you pause or restart the tour

## How to use it

1. Read the card on Home. It always has a "What is it" sentence and a "Try it" or "Show me" call to action.
2. Tap **Show me** to open the relevant screen, or **Tell me more** to have ORB explain it in one minute.
3. Tap **Skip** to mark the card seen without acting; Vitana won't surface it again.
4. To stop the tour entirely, open Settings → Preferences and toggle "Did You Know" off. You can re-enable it anytime.
5. Voice flow: when ORB asks "Did you know you can X?", say "yes" for the explanation, "later" to defer one day, or "no thanks" to dismiss the topic.
